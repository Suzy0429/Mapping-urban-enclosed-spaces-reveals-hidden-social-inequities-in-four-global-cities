Mapping urban enclosed spaces reveals hidden social inequities in four global cities
===
Shujuan Tang, Mingxing Chen, Yaping Wang, Longwu Liang, Huiping Jiang, Yuwen Sui, Zilu Chen, Zhifu Mi
This repository contains the data and scripts for the paper《Mapping urban enclosed spaces reveals hidden social inequities in four global cities》.The authors are Shujuan Tang, Mingxing Chen, Yaping Wang, Longwu Liang, Huiping Jiang, Yuwen Sui, Zilu Chen, Zhifu Mi.

Abstract
---
Urban enclosed spaces are widespread across cities worldwide and profoundly shape urban inclusivity, resource allocation, and social equity. Yet existing research lacks a unified framework for identifying and quantifying urban enclosed spaces (UES); as a result, the "filtering effect" exerted by physical boundaries on resource flows has long been underestimated. Here we develop an automated framework that integrates street-view semantic segmentation with semantic-enhanced classification to identify parcel-level UES at the city scale and to distinguish their functional types. We apply this framework to Beijing, London, New York, and Mumbai—four cities at distinct developmental stages and within distinct institutional contexts—and produce fine-grained cross-city maps of UES distribution. Across the four cities, UES differ markedly in scale, functional composition, and spatial pattern, with area shares ranging from 2% to 46%. We further construct a field model of enclosed space that captures the interaction between physical and social space to examine how enclosed parcels shape resource allocation and social equity. The results show that cities with similar degrees of enclosure can nonetheless exhibit sharply different spatial and resource-organizational modes. By turning UES into a comparable dimension for assessing urban exclusion and spatial inequality, this framework is scalable to the city level and provides a new tool for empirical research on urban equity in global cities.
